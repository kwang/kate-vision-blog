I keep hearing the same panic echoing around the tech industry right now: *"AI is coming for our jobs."*

But if we’re being brutally honest with ourselves... look at what those jobs actually were. For the last two decades, we’ve been forcing ourselves to work like machines. We memorized syntax. We spent hours debugging boilerplate code. We optimized our daily routines just to process endless Jira tickets faster. We essentially turned our brains into highly stressed, carbon-based compilers. 

We were doing machine work. 

Now, silicon is finally good enough to do the silicon work. AI can write the boilerplate. It can summarize that miserable 50-email thread. It can spot the missing semicolon. 

And honestly? Good. 

**We aren’t being replaced. We are being liberated.** 

By offloading the robotic parts of our day to AI, we finally get our bandwidth back. We get to be human again. 

When execution becomes cheap and instant, your *taste* and *judgment* become your most valuable assets. When an AI can give you a thousand perfect answers in seconds, the only thing that actually matters is your ability to ask the *right question*. 

As engineering leaders, our job isn't to mourn the loss of writing repetitive code. Our job is to look at our teams and ask: What kind of impossible things can we build now that we finally have the time, the mental space, and the tools to actually dream?

It’s time to start creating.
