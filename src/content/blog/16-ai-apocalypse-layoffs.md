Just as I finished writing about the death of the resume and the need for humans to become "Designated Risk-Absorbers," the news broke this morning: **Block (formerly Square) just axed 40% of its workforce—over 4,000 employees.** 

The official corporate reasoning? They are leveraging AI to build "smaller, more agile teams." 

Let’s translate that corporate BS into engineering reality. 

### The End of the "Code Monkey" Era

For the last decade, Silicon Valley has operated like a bloated factory. If a project was moving too slowly, executives would just throw more headcounts at the problem. We hired thousands of junior and mid-level developers just to write boilerplate code, move data from API A to API B, and manually resolve Jira tickets. 

Today, Block essentially declared that an entire stratum of software engineering is dead. 

You do not need 4,000 engineers to maintain a payment architecture if 80% of their work can be reliably executed by a finetuned Llama or Claude instance. Block isn't just cutting costs; they are redefining what a software company looks like. 

### The "Smaller Teams" Lie

When executives say "AI enables smaller teams," what they actually mean is: **"We finally realized we were paying $200k a year for people to do what a $0.02 API call can do."**

This is the great culling I’ve been talking about. The people who got fired today were the "Ticket Crunchers"—the ones who treated software engineering like an assembly line. They had flawless execution for menial tasks, but they lacked the architectural vision to see that their entire job description was highly automatable. 

### Who Survived the Purge?

The 60% who kept their jobs at Block today are not necessarily the best coders. They are the **Stable Interfaces** and the **Designated Risk-Absorbers**. 

They are the Senior Architects who know how to plug these new AI models into the chaotic, 15-year-old legacy codebase without bringing down the global payment network. They are the Engineering Managers who understand the deeply illogical, messy business requirements that an AI can never parse. 

They kept the people who hold the ultimate liability. They fired the typists. 

If you are still optimizing your resume by memorizing LeetCode algorithms, wake up. The asteroid just hit. You either learn how to command the silicon monkeys, or you get replaced by them.

**突发：Block 狂裁 40% 员工——“小团队”的谎言已经开始**

就在我刚写完“简历已死”、人类必须成为“指定风险吸收器”的文章时，今天早上的突发新闻直接把理论变成了极其血腥的现实：**Block（前身为 Square）刚刚一口气裁掉了 40% 的员工——超过 4000 人。**

官方的公关说辞是什么？他们正在利用 AI 来组建“更小、更敏捷的团队”。

让我们把这套恶心的企业废话翻译成真实的工程现状。

### “代码猴子”时代的终结

在过去的十年里，硅谷的运作模式就像一家极其臃肿的血汗工厂。如果一个项目进展太慢，高管们的解决方案就是疯狂加人头（Headcounts）。我们雇佣了成千上万的初级和中级开发人员，仅仅是为了让他们写模板代码（Boilerplate）、把数据从 API A 搬到 API B，以及手动清理无聊的 Jira 工单。

今天，Block 实质上宣布了：软件工程的某一个完整阶层，已经彻底死亡。

如果 80% 的日常工作都可以被一个微调过的 Llama 或 Claude 实例极其可靠地执行，你根本不需要 4000 个工程师来维护一个支付架构。Block 不仅仅是在削减成本；他们正在重新定义一家软件公司到底应该长什么样。

### “小团队”的谎言

当高管们说“AI 赋能了更小的团队”时，他们的潜台词其实是：**“我们终于他妈的意识到，我们以前竟然花着 20 万美元的年薪，雇人去干一次只要 2 美分的 API 调用就能干完的事。”**

这就是我一直提到的大清洗（The Great Culling）。今天被解雇的那些人，就是典型的“无情碎票机（Ticket Crunchers）”——那些把软件工程当成流水线操作工的人。他们对琐碎任务有着完美的执行力，但他们缺乏架构级的视野（Vision），根本没意识到自己的整个职位描述都已经高度可自动化了。

### 谁在清洗中活了下来？

今天在 Block 保住饭碗的那 60% 的人，绝对不是写代码最快的人。他们是**“稳定的接口”**和**“指定风险吸收器”**。

他们是那些懂得如何把这些新一代 AI 模型，强行塞进拥有 15 年历史的、混乱不堪的祖传代码库里，同时保证全球支付网络不宕机的资深架构师。他们是那些能够理解连 AI 都无法解析的、极其反逻辑和混乱的商业需求的研发经理（Engineering Manager）。

公司留下了那些能够承担终极法律和业务责任的人。他们开除了打字员。

如果你现在还在靠死记硬背 LeetCode 算法题来优化你的简历，醒醒吧。小行星已经撞下来了。你要么学会如何指挥这群硅基猴子，要么，你就被它们取代。
